Aliasing


What is aliasing?

In the most straightforward terms, aliasing is a type of artifact in digital audio when the original analog signal gets misidentified by the digital system. Currently, widely used GAN-based vocoders attain waveform via upsampling the mel-spectrogram. This processing can be viewed as "adding features and harmonics." Several works have pointed out that the upsampling block and the activation functions will bring out the aliasing problem in audio engineering.

What does it sound like?


Audio with strong aliasing effects will tend to be metallic noise since all the harmonic parts are stuck together.
