Poor High Frequency Reconstruction


What does it sound like?

It is hard for current neural vocoders to resynthesis the high-frequency parts accurately. The resulting audio usually sounds "dull" and "dark," even with audible white noise in the high-frequency components. Below are two audio samples. The first is the ground truth, and the second is the prediction.

A closer look.

Here are two audio samples with a sampling rate of 24khz passed through an equalizer, as shown in the figure above. The first is the ground truth, and the second is our vocoder's prediction result. For ground truth, the high-frequency components are precise and thus contribute to the whole audio's expressiveness. For prediction, on the other hand, the resynthesis inaccuracy in high-frequency parts makes it noisy and dull. Therefore the prediction audio will sound less light compared with the ground truth.
