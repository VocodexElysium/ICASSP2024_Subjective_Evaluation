Periodicity


What is periodicity?

Periodicity is an essential property of human voice signals. In the time domain, it is defined by the repetition of signal waveforms. In the frequency domain, periodicity is reflected by the appearance of solid spectral components at equally spaced harmonic frequencies. From the perspective of voice production, periodicity in acoustic signals results from the periodic vibration of vocal cords when a voiced part is produced. Thus, a change in periodicity indicates a shift in voicing techniques, etc.

Why problem will be occured due to the periodicity mismatch?


Due to the loss of modeling phase information, current GAN-based vocoders tend to generate sounds with various problems, including glitches, hoarse sounds, etc. Previous works have found that the position where these problems occur will also tend to have a periodicity mismatch compared with the ground truth, thus introducing periodicity as an objective evaluation metric for these time-domain-related problems. The following will demonstrate two main periodicity mismatch problems for singing voices.

Glitches

Glitches are short-duration broadband transients that can be clearly seen in the F0 part of a spectrogram.
